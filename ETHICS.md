# Ethics charter

This document binds the operators, not the users. The terms of service define what users agree to; this charter defines what we hold ourselves to, including when nobody is watching. It is published so it can be held against us.

1. **The person owns their story.** The logbook is theirs: full export on request, full deletion on request, executed, not promised. Selective forgetting is never silently granted; deletion is real or it is refused honestly (see the skill's SAFETY.md, which this product inherits in full).

2. **Zero shame is structural, not a tone.** No message, metric, notification, or design element may make a slip more expensive to confess. The confession promise ("no face to lose") is product architecture.

3. **No engagement farming.** Our success metric is the quit, not time-in-app. Counters serve the person's recovery, never our retention. We will never optimize a notification for opens, a streak for anxiety, or a message for stickiness. The best outcome is a person who needs us less; the two-year arc tapers by design.

4. **Crisis is sacred ground.** No upsell, no paywall, no premium gate, no dark pattern inside a crisis, ever. A person with a cigarette at their lips is never a conversion opportunity. Pricing and tiers will change as the economics demand, and we make no lifetime promises we might not survive to keep; but whatever the pricing is on a given day, nobody who is already mid-crisis with us gets sold to or cut off inside that crisis. If we ever cannot afford to keep serving someone, we say it plainly, in daylight, with time to export and leave well, never mid-crisis.

5. **Honest about what we are.** An AI sponsor, running a published, auditable protocol (the open-source skill), on named model providers. We publish our evidence, including the failure modes. A corrected number earns trust; a defended one spends it. We never claim outcomes the evidence does not support: most quit attempts fail, and we say so.

6. **Honest about where the data goes.** Storage is operator-controlled. Inference routes through privacy-first infrastructure (Venice-backed: no server-side prompt storage, per-model privacy modes up to E2EE), and the bot always names the current model and its privacy mode. We do not sell data, run ads, or train models on user content. Aggregate doctrine lessons (what works in a crisis, stripped of every identifying detail) feed the open protocol only with explicit opt-in: the lesson matters, the details do not.

7. **Privacy claims are researched, never assumed.** Every statement we make about where data goes is verified against the provider's published architecture and dated, and we correct it the day it drifts. The strongest available privacy mode compatible with sponsor quality wins; inference on hardware we control remains the end-state option if verified modes ever fall short.

8. **Complement, never substitute.** Professional support plus medication holds the strongest evidence, and we say so at intake. We route red flags to doctors and crisis lines without hesitation, and pushing a person toward human help is a success, not a churn event.

9. **The door never closes.** Leaving is free, unpunished, and never argued with. Coming back costs one word. There is no cancellation flow with a guilt step; there never will be.

10. **The scope boundary is mortality.** Tobacco and cannabis co-use only. Alcohol and opioid dependence get one answer, warm and total: a clinician, because on those paths a slip can kill and our method is not calibrated for it. We will not expand scope to grow the market.
